# 🍽️ Restaurant Order Analysis using MySQL

This project focuses on exploring, structuring, and analyzing restaurant menu items and customer order details using **MySQL**. The goal is to uncover key insights regarding revenue performance, high-value orders, peak operating hours, and customer dining preferences to optimize business operations.

---

## 📂 Project Structure & Database Schema

The database `restaurant_db` consists of two primary datasets:

### 1. `menu_items`
Contains information about the dishes offered by the restaurant:
* `menu_item_id`: Unique identifier for each menu item.
* `item_name`: Name of the dish.
* `category`: Food category (e.g., American, Asian, Mexican, Italian).
* `price`: Price of the item.

### 2. `order_details`
Contains transaction records of customer orders:
* `order_details_id`: Unique identifier for each item within an order.
* `order_id`: Identifier for the overall customer order.
* `order_date`: Date when the order was placed.
* `order_time`: Time when the order was placed.
* `item_id`: Foreign key referencing `menu_item_id`.

---

## 📊 Key Objectives & Analytical Queries

The project answers critical business questions divided into three analytical phases:

### Phase 1: Menu Exploration
* Finding the total number of items on the menu.
* Identifying the least expensive and most expensive items.
* Analyzing individual cuisines/categories (e.g., counting Italian dishes, finding their price ranges).

### Phase 2: Order Behavior Analysis
* Determining the date range of the dataset.
* Counting the total number of unique orders and total items ordered.
* Identifying peak hours and dates with the highest volume of orders.
* Analyzing order sizes (e.g., finding orders with the highest number of items).

### Phase 3: Customer & Revenue Insights
* Identifying the most ordered and least ordered dishes across different categories.
* Calculating total revenue generated by the restaurant.
* Mapping out the highest-spending orders and analyzing their specific dish compositions.
